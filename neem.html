What is Neem?

Neem is a tree in the mahogany family Meliaceae. It is native to the Indian subcontinent, but now grows in tropical regions around the world. The tree has been used for centuries for its medicinal properties and is often referred to as the "wonder tree."

Neem is used for a wide range of ailments, including skin diseases, fever, infections, and digestive problems. It is also used as an insecticide and pesticide, and has been shown to have antifungal, antibacterial, and antiviral properties.
