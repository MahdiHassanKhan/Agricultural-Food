What is Ghost Pepper?

Ghost Pepper, also known as Bhut Jolokia, is a chili pepper native to Northeast India. It is widely cultivated in the states of Assam, Nagaland, and Manipur. The pepper is known for its extreme heat and is one of the hottest peppers in the world.

The heat of ghost pepper is due to the presence of capsaicin, a compound that stimulates pain receptors in the mouth and skin. It is used in cooking to add heat and flavor to dishes, but it should be used with caution due to its intense spiciness.
